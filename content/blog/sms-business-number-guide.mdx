If you run a service business in Texas — HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing — your phone number is one of your most important marketing assets. 

But here's a question most business owners never think to ask: **Can your customers text you?**

If the answer is no, you're quietly losing business every single day. Here's why it matters, what it's costing you, and how to fix it in under 15 minutes.

## The Texting Revolution Is Already Here

Think about how you communicate with your own service providers. When you need a plumber, do you want to call and sit through a phone tree, or would you rather send a quick text saying "Can someone come by Tuesday morning?"

Consumers overwhelmingly prefer the second option. According to recent data, **9 out of 10 consumers want to be able to text businesses**. That's not a niche preference — it's the standard expectation across every demographic.

For home service businesses, this is especially critical. When someone's AC goes out in a Texas summer, they're stressed and impatient. A text message feels immediate and low-friction. A phone call feels like a hassle.

## What Happens When You Can't Receive Texts

Your Google Business Profile displays your phone number. When a potential customer finds you on Google, they have two options:

1. **Call you** — which requires them to stop what they're doing, make a call, talk to a person, and potentially wait on hold
2. **Move on to a competitor** — who may have a "Text" button right next to their phone number on Google

Google actually makes this easy to see. If your number is SMS-capable, GBP shows a "Text" button. If not, only the "Call" button appears. When both options are available, customers overwhelmingly choose text.

The result? Businesses with text-enabled numbers capture more leads — especially the busy, distracted customers who are ready to buy but don't have time for a phone call.

## The Real Cost of Not Having SMS

Let's do some simple math.

Say you get 100 potential customers viewing your Google listing per month. Even if only 10% of them would prefer to text rather than call, that's **10 missed opportunities per month**.

If your average job value is $300-500, that's **$3,000 to $5,000 in potential monthly revenue walking out the door** — not because your service isn't great, but because you made it slightly harder for people to reach you.

Over a year, that's $36,000 to $60,000 in lost revenue. For something that takes 15 minutes to set up and costs $0-15 per month.

## How to Fix It (15 Minutes, Free or Cheap)

The fix is straightforward:

**Option 1: Google Voice (Free)**
Create a free Google Voice number. It supports SMS by default. Forward texts to your personal phone. Update your Google Business Profile with the new number. Done.

**Option 2: SMS-Enabled Business Line ($5-15/month)**
Most VoIP providers offer SMS-enabled numbers. GoHighLevel, RingCentral, and Grasshopper all include texting on their basic plans.

**Option 3: Enable SMS on Your Current Number**
Some carriers can enable SMS on existing landline-style numbers. Call your provider and ask.

After setting it up, make sure to:
- Update your Google Business Profile with the SMS-enabled number
- Update all your online directory listings (Yelp, Facebook, Bing, etc.)
- Add "Text us at [number]" to your website and email signature

## The Anatomy of a Winning Nurture Sequence

A complete nurture system for a home service business has four core sequences:

### 1. Post-Service Follow-Up (Days 1-14)

This sequence starts the moment your technician closes the job and collects payment.

**Day 1: Thank You + Review Request (Email + SMS)**

The customer just paid. Your work is fresh in their mind. Strike while the iron is hot.

*Subject: Thanks for choosing [Company Name], [First Name]!*

"Hi [First Name], thank you for trusting us with your [service] today. We hope everything is working perfectly. If you have a moment, we'd really appreciate a quick review: [Review Link]. Your feedback helps us serve our Texas neighbors better."

Keep the SMS version short: "Thanks for choosing us today! We'd love a 5-star review: [Link]. Reply STOP to opt out."

**Day 3: Service Confirmation + Maintenance Tip (Email)**

Reinforce that they made a good choice and provide useful info.

"3 tips to extend the life of your new [equipment/service]: 1) [Tip], 2) [Tip], 3) [Tip]. We're here if you need anything."

**Day 14: Referral Request (Email + SMS)**

The most underutilized lead source: happy customers with friends.

"Know someone who needs [service]? We offer a $25 credit for every referral that books a job with us. Just send them to [Referral Link]."

### 2. Seasonal Maintenance Reminders (Ongoing)

This is the backbone of your nurture system. Texas has distinct service seasons, and your sequences should align.

**Spring (March-April): Pre-Summer AC Tune-Up**

"Texas summers are brutal. Beat the heat — and the emergency service prices — by scheduling your pre-season AC maintenance now. Members save 15% on tune-ups. [Booking Link]"

**Summer (June-August): AC Performance Check + Filter Reminder**

"Running your AC hard this summer? When's the last time you changed your filter? We'll send you a reminder every 90 days — or you can set up auto-delivery: [Filter Subscription Link]"

**Fall (September-October): Furnace + Heater Prep**

"Texas winters might not last long, but they can be tough on your furnace. Schedule your heating system check before the first cold snap. [Booking Link]"

**Winter (November-February): Pipe Protection + Freeze Alerts**

"Temperatures dropping tonight? Here's how to protect your pipes from freezing. And if the worst happens, we're on call 24/7: [Emergency Number]"

**Bonus: Post-Storm Follow-Up**

After major weather events (hail, flooding, ice storms), send a targeted sequence to customers in affected areas.

"We're checking on customers in [Affected Area] after last night's storm. If you have roof, gutter, or electrical damage, call us for a free inspection."

### 3. Membership/Service Agreement Nurture

In 2026, service agreements (maintenance plans) are the most profitable product a home service business offers. Your nurture sequence should aggressively promote them.

**Touchpoint 1: Service Agreement Benefits**

"Did you know our maintenance plan members get priority scheduling, 15% off repairs, and annual tune-ups for a low monthly fee? Here's what it could save you this year…"

**Touchpoint 2: Expiration/Upcoming Renewal**

"Your maintenance plan expires in 30 days. Renew now to keep your benefits active: [Renewal Link]"

**Touchpoint 3: Lapsed Member Win-Back**

"We miss you! As a former member, you can rejoin at your original rate for the next 7 days. [Rejoin Link]"

### 4. Re-Engagement Sequence (Lapsed Customers)

For customers who haven't booked service in 12+ months, a dedicated re-engagement sequence can recover 10-20% of them.

**Email 1 (Month 12): "It's been a while"**

"Hi [First Name], it's been about a year since we serviced your [equipment]. We'd love to help you stay ahead of maintenance. Here's $25 off your next service: [Coupon Link]"

**Email 2 (Month 13): Industry Update**

"Here's what's new in home [trade] in 2026 that could save you money: [Link to blog/video]. When you're ready, we're here."

**Email 3 (Month 15): Final Offer**

"Last chance for your $25 discount — it expires this week. Don't let a small issue turn into a big (expensive) problem. [Booking Link]"

Email and SMS marketing for service businesses performs differently than general retail or e-commerce. Here's what typical Texas home service businesses see in 2026:

| Email open rate | 28-35% | 40-50% |

| Opt-out rate (SMS) | 0.5-1% per send | 0.2-0.5% |

SMS consistently outperforms email for home service businesses — often by 3-5x on click-through rates. The reason is simple: homeowners check their text messages within minutes, while emails can sit unread for days. ### The Optimal Channel Mix

For Texas service businesses, the best approach is a combination:

- **Transactional messages** (appointment reminders, dispatch updates, post-service thanks): SMS
- **Review requests**: SMS (higher completion rate)
- **Seasonal reminders**: Both — SMS for urgency, email for detail
- **Educational content** (tips, videos, blogs): Email
- **Emergency alerts** (freeze warnings, storm response): SMS
- **Referral requests**: Both — email with details, SMS with link

## Building Your List: Permission Is Everything

A nurture sequence is only as good as your contact list. Here's how to build it ethically and effectively:

- **Collect email and phone at booking**: Make it a required field when customers book service
- **Obtain clear opt-in**: "Can we text you appointment reminders and seasonal tips? Standard message rates apply."
- **Use the post-service capture**: After the job, confirm the customer's preferred contact method
- **Offer an incentive**: "Get $25 off your next service when you sign up for our email list"
- **Include in every touchpoint**: Estimate confirmations, invoices, receipts, website forms
- **Comply with TCPA**: Get written consent for SMS marketing. The Telephone Consumer Protection Act is strictly enforced in 2026, and fines can reach $1,500 per violation.

## Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

### Mistake 1: Too Many Emails, Too Fast

Nothing kills a nurture sequence faster than overwhelming new customers. One email per week is plenty for the first month. Monthly touchpoints are sufficient for ongoing maintenance.

### Mistake 2: All Sell, No Value

If every message is asking for a booking, customers will tune out. Follow the 80/20 rule: 80% value (tips, education, local info) and 20% promotional.

### Mistake 3: Not Segmenting Your List

A customer who just had their AC replaced doesn't need the same messages as someone who had a drain cleaning. Segment by service type, equipment age, and lifecycle stage.

### Mistake 4: Ignoring SMS Compliance

SMS marketing has strict rules. You must provide opt-in confirmation, easy opt-out (reply STOP), and clear sender identification. Violating TCPA rules can result in class-action lawsuits.

### Mistake 5: No Way to Track Results

If you're not tracking open rates, click rates, unsubscribe rates, and — most importantly — booked jobs from email/SMS, you're flying blind. Every platform exports these metrics. Use them.
